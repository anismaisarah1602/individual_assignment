Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug, third from the left, trains biologists in Mexico on how to increase wheat yields - part of his life-long war on hunger.

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1914 – Born in Cresco, Iowa.
  • 1933 – Leaves his family’s farm to attend the University of Minnesota during the Great Depression.
  • 1944 – Begins research in Mexico, working on improving crop production and fighting hunger.
  • 1960s – Leads the Green Revolution, helping countries like India and Pakistan avoid famine by increasing food production.
  • 1970 – Receives the Nobel Peace Prize for his contributions to fighting global hunger.
  • 1984 – Becomes a distinguished professor at Texas A&M University.
  • 2005 – Advocates for increasing food production to sustain the growing global population.
  • 2009 – Dies at the age of 95, leaving a legacy of saving over a billion lives.
"Borlaug's life and achievement are testimony to the far-reaching contribution that one man's towering intellect, persistence and scientific vision can make to human peace and progress."

-- Indian Prime Minister Manmohan Singh
